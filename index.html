Your doctor most likely checked total testosterone — which includes both bound and unbound hormone. The problem is that bound testosterone can't be used by your tissues. What matters is free testosterone: the fraction your body can actually act on. A man with "normal" total testosterone and high SHBG can be functionally deficient. Most standard panels don't measure this. Ours tells you exactly what to test and what the numbers mean.

SHBG (Sex Hormone-Binding Globulin) is a protein that binds to testosterone and makes it unavailable for use. Think of it as testosterone that's been locked up. High SHBG means less free testosterone — even if your total testosterone looks fine on paper. SHBG is heavily influenced by lifestyle factors: alcohol, sleep, body fat, thyroid function, and more. It's one of the most important and most commonly skipped markers.

YOU'VE BEEN LIED TO ABOUT "NORMAL"

You probably haven't thought about your testosterone in years. Why would you? You're not a bodybuilder. You're not on steroids. You're just... a guy. Working. Living. Getting by.

But somewhere along the way, things shifted. The energy dipped. The sharpness faded. The mornings got heavier. You didn't notice it happen because it didn't happen overnight — it happened one percent at a time.

And if you ever brought it up, you got the same answer: "It's normal for your age."

Here's what that actually means: your doctor checked your total testosterone. It was technically in range. Case closed.

But total testosterone is like gross income. 100K a month sounds great — until your expenses are 98K. What you actually live on is the 2K that's left. Your total testosterone is the 100K. Your free testosterone — the part your body can actually use — is what you're living on. And that number isn't determined by age. It's determined by how you live.

You're not declining. You're uninformed. There's a difference. And it's a fixable one.
